About Me

Greetings! I am Amy Austin. I was born in Ann Arbor, Michigan. I've lived and worked in Colorado for the past eighteen years. Originally, I earned an undergraduate degree in Education and Humanities. I had been in education for almost twenty years in Michigan and in Colorado.

A life goal of mine has always been to try to change the world, one student at a time, but realized that this was not far-reaching enough. About eight years ago, I began to wonder how else could I make an impact on the world? A concern of mine has always been the environment, and more specifically, climate change and human effects on the top of our land food chain, the polar bear. I traveled to Churchill to get a first hand look at human impact on the polar bear habitat. What I saw was alarming, no ice and wintry landscape when these conditions should have been present. I spoke with biologists at the Polar Bear Research facility about an idea I had to help the polar bears, but my idea was met with speculation. This is when I decided that I would go back to school to become an engineer to try to build my prototype, but would need a lot of coursework and background to accomplish this goal.

>I have always been passionate about technology and believe this is another avenue that will help with my prototype. I decided to research multiple coding bootcamps. I was impressed with University of Denver Bootcamp and decided to enroll. I am extremely happy that I did. Additionally, I am taking Calculus 3 and Intro to Python Programming at night. I would like to pursue employment in the web development field while attending Colorado School of Mines either full time or part time beginning in August 2018.
